Publications

High-End Ion Mobility Mass Spectrometry: A Current Review of Analytical Capacity in Omics Applications and Structural Investigations

Review of high-end ion mobility instrumentation and their suitability in metabolomics/proteomics investigations, as well as their capacity for structural elucidation.

Read
Cyclic immonium ion of lactyllysine reveals widespread lactylation in the human proteome

A novel diagnostic ion-based strategy, to confidently identify new lactylation events, unveiling a wide lactylproteome landscape that had previously been confined to lactylated histones.

Read
Complementary proteome and glycoproteome access revealed through comparative analysis of reversed phase and porous graphitic carbon chromatography

First reports of porous graphitic carbon for untargeted glycoproteomics compared to traditional reversed phase liquid chromatography

Read
Recent Advances in Analytical Approaches for Glycan and Glycopeptide Quantitation

Review of experimental considerations and advancements for quantitative glycomics and glycopeptidomics over the years 2010-2020.

Read
Recent developments and applications of quantitative proteomics strategies for high-throughput biomolecular analyses in cancer research

Review of recent applications and associated advancements in quantitative proteomics applications in cancer research.

Read
Proteome-wide and Matrisome-specific Alterations during Human Pancreas Development and Maturation

This comprehensive proteomics analysis contributes to an improved understanding of the critical roles that ECM plays throughout human pancreas development and maturation.

Read
Peroxymonosulfate Oxidizes Amino Acids in Water without Activation

Peroxymonosulfate is capable of oxidizing 19 amino acids without explicit activation and that solvent-exposed methionine and tryptophan residues are likely initial targets of oxidation in peptides and proteins.

Read
Improved Structural Elucidation of Peptide Isomers and Their Receptors Using Advanced Ion Mobility-Mass Spectrometry

We explore the usefulness of ion mobility techniques in probing structurally-critical biomolecules, examining the ability of current methods to elucidate neuropeptide stereoisomers and receptors.

Read
Finding Biomass Degrading Enzymes Through an Activity-Correlated Quantitative Proteomics Platform (ACPP)

The results demonstrated that the ACPP facilitated the discovery of bioactive enzymes from complex protein samples in a high-throughput, multiplexing, and untargeted fashion.
